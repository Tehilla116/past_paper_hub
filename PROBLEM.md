# Problem Statement — Past Paper Hub

## The Problem

Students at Zambia University of Technology lack a centralized, reliable way to access past examination papers. Currently, past papers are distributed through informal channels — lecturers share them via email, USB drives, or classroom groups, and students rely on word-of-mouth or personal networks to find them. This leads to:

- **Inconsistent access** — not all students can obtain the same materials
- **No central archive** — papers are scattered and easily lost
- **No quality control** — students cannot verify authenticity
- **Extra workload for lecturers** — repeatedly fulfilling individual requests for past papers

## Proposed Solution

Past Paper Hub is a full-stack web application that serves as the university's centralized past paper repository. Lecturers upload examination papers directly, and students browse, search, and download them through a structured interface organized by school, course, and year.

### Key Benefits

- **Equitable access** — every student with a login can download any available paper
- **Permanent archive** — papers persist in a structured database
- **Role-based management** — lecturers control uploads, admins oversee the system
- **Reduced lecturer burden** — upload once, available to all

## Technology

Built with React.js (frontend), Express.js (backend), and PostgreSQL (database) as the full-stack course project for the Zambia University of Technology.
